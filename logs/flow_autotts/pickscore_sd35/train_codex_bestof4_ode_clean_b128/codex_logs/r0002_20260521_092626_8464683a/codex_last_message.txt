**Findings**
- Beat ODE: `beta=0.0` and `0.25`.
- Did not beat ODE: `beta=0.5`, `0.75`, `1.0`.
- `r0000 -> r0001` was a real behavior change mainly at `beta=0.25` (single-root preview -> backward refinement); the rest were mostly threshold tuning.
- Prior compute was spent on early previews and full-root completions, then modest backward children, with little pruning.

**Schedule**
- `beta=0`: deterministic ODE finish, then `latest_active`.
- `0 < beta < 0.25`: 1 root, cheap scout, no branch.
- `0.25-0.54`: 2 roots, 1 branch anchor, 1 child, inferred noise.
- `0.55-0.84`: 3 roots, 2 branch anchors, 2 children, mixed noise, 1 top-off.
- `>=0.85`: 4 roots, 3 branch anchors, 3 children, 2 top-offs, later confirmation.
- Monotonicity: higher beta increases roots, confirm depth, branch count, child count, and willingness to spend late compute.

**Target-NFE Reflection**
- `beta=0.0`: justified early stop, no underuse.
- `beta=0.25`: previous controller likely underused/under-allocated; I expanded it with 2 roots and confirm/branch gating.
- `beta=0.5`: not mainly underuse; fixed allocation quality instead of adding blind compute.
- `beta=0.75`: previous controller looked under-spent versus its target; I added more breadth, confirm, and top-off.
- `beta=1.0`: same, strongest budget-upshift and late confirmation.
